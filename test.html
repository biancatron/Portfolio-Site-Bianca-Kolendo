“I wake up and immediately I’m busy. I don't get a lot of time to sit down and do something for me. Wake up, get ready, then go to my job. Sometimes I get breaks during the day.. but not usually.”

Proto-Persona Problem Statement

Murphy needs a way to effortlessly study new words, because she is working full time and is the sole caretaker for her daughter.
We will know this to be true when Murphy is interacting with the app daily.

Research + Ideation

When conducting a competitive analysis, the most frustrating and distracting part of the learning process was having too much functionality. On the flipside, I also ran into having far too little. There needs to be a balance to allow users to focus on what they want to accomplish, which is studying new vocabulary, but still allow the user to challenge themselves with more than just a word and definition. Get in and get it done; instead of get in, navigate to what you’re looking for, or make extraneous decisions.

  1. In this first example, from an app called Quizlet, I found there was too much functionality. When I wanted to create a new card set, there were three options for getting starting, with no explanation. What’s the difference between a set, folder, and class?
  2. Next, we have a screenshot from Vocabulary app. This screenshot is basically all the app does. It shows you both the word and definition, eliminating that practice needed to build neuron connections for vocabulary recall.
  3. Finally, we see our Goldilocks example from the Vocabulary Builder app. It’s a good balance of simplicity with the challenge of recalling a definition.

Usability Testing

For this usability test, I created a short testing plan. This included a script to maintain consistency between sessions and scenario tasks for my participants to complete. These tasks tested the functionality of:

  • Vocabulary word creation
  • Reviewing words and definitions
  • Notification setting

It was interesting to see where I provided too much context and where I didn’t provide enough. The most severe problem was the 3 onboarding screens, but there were opportunities throughout the app.

Reflection + Conclusion

I learned that I underestimated the users. I quickly realized the benefits of working quickly and being messy first, and then cleaning up sketches as the project progressed. It was surprisingly easy to get feedback on my designs without feeling like it was for myself as the designer. The key feedback I took was that the use of language is very important. There were a few inconsistencies I used which led to confusion- like moving from “set” to “topic” without clarification.
Going forward, I will need to work on softening my delivery, while still providing clear, concise direction to my participants. Overall, I could improve my design process by giving each step more time for variations and reviewing for consistency.
